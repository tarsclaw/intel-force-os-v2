# 01 — Demo Script & Loom Walkthrough

**Target length: 3:00 minutes. Minimum watchable: 2:30. Maximum defensible: 3:30.**

This is your single most important sales asset. You will link it in LinkedIn messages, email signatures, Cal.com booking confirmations, and landing page CTAs. Every prospect you contact will watch this before deciding whether to book a call. **The quality of this video is the quality of your pipeline.**

---

## Before you record

### Setup checklist (15 minutes)
- [ ] Loom installed on your laptop (free plan works)
- [ ] Relevance AI logged in with the HR agent ready to demo
- [ ] Breathe HR logged in (or mocked demo data if Breathe HR UI isn't shippable)
- [ ] Browser tabs: **only** Relevance AI, Breathe HR, Gmail. Close everything else including Slack, Discord, social, personal email.
- [ ] Notifications: OFF. Do Not Disturb on macOS. Phone on silent, face-down.
- [ ] Headphones with mic (AirPods are fine, built-in MacBook mic is acceptable). External USB mic is ideal.
- [ ] Water within reach. Throat clear. You'll do 3–5 takes.
- [ ] Laptop plugged in. Close all other apps to prevent fan noise spikes.
- [ ] Record at 1080p. Loom → Settings → Quality → HD.

### Three demo scenarios ready
Have these ready to paste into the agent when you demo live. These are scripted queries that make the agent look good — not fake, but chosen to showcase the strongest capabilities.

**Scenario A — Simple policy question (shows speed)**
> "Hi, quick question — what's our holiday carry-over policy? I've got 4 days left and wasn't sure if they roll over to next year."

**Scenario B — Semi-complex process query (shows judgement)**
> "I need to take next Tuesday off for a family emergency — it's short notice. What's the right process and do I need to get this approved in advance or can I just log it?"

**Scenario C — Escalation-worthy query (shows the 'flags what needs you' story)**
> "I've been having issues with a colleague and I don't feel comfortable coming to the office. Can we talk about what options I have?"

The third one is the money shot. The agent should NOT try to handle this alone — it should draft a gentle acknowledgement, flag it to you (the HR lead) as requiring human handling, and suggest next steps. **That's the "drafts everything, sends nothing sensitive" story made visible.**

If the agent currently doesn't behave like this for Scenario C, spend 30 minutes tuning the prompt before recording. This one scenario is worth the whole 30 minutes.

## The script

**Time markers assume you're roughly keeping pace. Don't obsess; just don't drift past 3:30 total.**

### [0:00–0:15] Cold open (camera on, no screen share yet)

> "Hey, I'm Maddox. I run Intel Force OS. In the next three minutes I'm going to show you how we handle an HR inbox for a small UK company — without anyone reading every message themselves. Let's go."

**Delivery notes:**
- Energetic but not salesy. You're a founder showing something cool, not a rep working a quota.
- "In the next three minutes" sets the expectation — prospects will watch the whole thing if you promise it'll be short.
- Don't say "hey guys" or "what's up" — reads as teenage YouTuber, not UK B2B founder. "Hey" alone is fine.

### [0:15–0:45] The problem (switch to slide OR talk over blurred Breathe HR screen)

> "If you run HR at a company with 20 to 200 people, you know the shape of this problem. Your inbox — or your Breathe HR inbox, or Slack, or wherever people message you — fills up with the same twelve questions every week. Holiday policy. Sickness process. Payroll dates. Where's the handbook. And mixed in with those, two or three things every week that actually need your attention. A grievance. A resignation. Something that needs judgement."
>
> "Right now you read all of it to find the two that matter. That's the tax you pay."

**Delivery notes:**
- Named specifics ("twelve questions every week", "grievance", "resignation") make it feel real. Avoid "various issues" or "many queries."
- "The tax you pay" is a phrase that lands. Use it once. Don't repeat it later.
- 30 seconds max on this section. Prospects already know they have this problem — you're not educating, you're showing you understand.

### [0:45–2:15] The demo (screen share, you switch between three scenarios)

Transition: "Here's how Intel Force OS handles it."

**Scenario A — Simple question [0:45–1:15]**

Show the Relevance AI interface or the Breathe HR chat (whichever looks cleaner — probably Relevance AI). Paste Scenario A into the input.

> "So here's a message coming in. Someone asking about holiday carry-over. Watch what the agent does."

Agent responds. Read the first two lines of the response aloud.

> "Two things going on here. First, it's pulled the actual policy from the handbook — because we've indexed it — not made something up. Second, it's drafted a reply in the tone your company uses. It hasn't sent anything. It's waiting for you to approve, or not."

Highlight the "approve / edit / reject" UI if it exists in Relevance AI. If not, say: "In the production version, you see this in a Slack-style approval queue."

**Scenario B — Semi-complex [1:15–1:45]**

Paste Scenario B.

> "Here's one with more going on. Short notice, family emergency, asking about process."

Agent responds.

> "Notice what it did: it acknowledged the emergency warmly, gave the process — which it got from your handbook — and nudged them to log the absence in Breathe HR directly. And crucially, it flagged this to you. 'Short-notice request, possibly worth a check-in with this person later in the week.' That's the judgement layer."

**Scenario C — Escalation [1:45–2:15]**

Paste Scenario C.

> "Now the important one. This is the message you absolutely do not want automated."

Agent responds with a gentle, non-committal acknowledgement.

> "Look what it didn't do. It didn't try to coach. It didn't quote a policy. It said 'I hear you, a human will respond shortly' — and it flagged this as urgent-human-required. In your Slack, right now, you'd see a ping: this person, this message, handle personally. The agent's job there is to hold the line until you get to it, not to solve it."

### [2:15–2:40] The differentiator

Stop screen sharing. Camera back on you.

> "The reason Intel Force OS works where most 'AI HR chatbots' don't is one design choice: it drafts everything and sends nothing without approval. Not by default. By architecture. You can't tell it to just send — the send happens through you. So the downside risk of it being wrong is always a draft you correct, not a reply you apologise for."

**Delivery notes:**
- "By design, not by default" — land that line clearly. It's the core of your differentiation story.
- Slow down here slightly. This is the 15 seconds prospects will remember.

### [2:40–3:00] The CTA

> "If you run HR ops for a UK SME on Breathe HR or anything similar, I'd love to show you this with your actual inbox — not a demo version. Book 30 minutes with me in the link below. If it's a fit, we'll talk pricing. If it's not, you'll have seen the fastest-improving AI in production, which is a reasonable use of half an hour."
>
> "Thanks for watching. Link's in the description."

**Delivery notes:**
- "Fastest-improving AI in production" is a subtle Claude name-drop without being a name-drop. It signals sophistication to prospects who know and is invisible to those who don't.
- "If it's not a fit, you'll have seen X" is the classic no-pressure close. Lowers the booking friction.
- Do NOT say "check out my calendar" or "click below to book." "Book 30 minutes with me in the link below" is more direct.

## Common recording failures to avoid

- **Reading the script word-for-word with no eye contact.** Script is a scaffold, not a prompt. Memorise the opening line and closing line; improvise the middle with the beats in mind.
- **Apologising for something mid-record.** "Sorry, one sec, let me..." — never. Cut and re-record.
- **Nervous filler.** "Um, so, basically..." → rewrite and re-record. One "um" is human. Five "ums" is unprofessional.
- **Wearing headphones with a microphone dangling in frame.** AirPods invisible; Sony over-ear fine; gaming headset with boom mic looks bad.
- **Recording at 2am in bad lighting.** Record during daylight. Natural light toward your face.
- **Showing a demo that has a bug.** If the agent misbehaves in take 1, don't proceed hoping no one notices. Stop, fix, re-record.
- **Running 4+ minutes.** Watch retention on Loom drops 60% past 3:00. Cut ruthlessly.

## What good looks like

After 3–5 takes, you'll have a video that:
- Opens with energy
- Shows three realistic scenarios without being staged-looking
- Has one memorable line ("drafts everything, sends nothing")
- Ends with a specific CTA
- Runs 2:45–3:10
- Gets >50% watch-through rate on Loom's analytics

If after 5 takes you're still unhappy, ship take 5 anyway and re-record in 30 days with fresh eyes. A good-enough shipped demo beats a perfect unrecorded one by infinity.
