## What this pack is NOT

- **Not another platform spec.** You have 150 documents of those. They're waiting. This pack is about what happens before that infrastructure matters.
- **Not a substitute for sending the first message.** You don't need to have read all six files before you can DM someone on LinkedIn today. If you only read the outreach templates and send 5 messages, that's a better day than reading all six and sending zero.
- **Not a marketing funnel.** This is founder-led sales to cold prospects. When you have 5+ customers, a funnel becomes worth building. Before that, it's cosplay.
- **Not a real prospect list.** The prospecting framework tells you how to build the list, with criteria and examples. You do the research. Two reasons: (a) I can't see LinkedIn or real company data, (b) doing the research yourself makes you better at it.

## The 30-day execution plan

**Week 1 — Asset creation (10–12 hours)**
- Day 1–2: Record the Loom demo (artifact 1)
- Day 2: Deploy the landing page (artifact 2)
- Day 3: Set up Cal.com booking link, connect to landing page
- Day 4–5: Build the 20-prospect list using the framework (artifact 3)

**Week 2 — Outreach (6–8 hours)**
- Day 6: Send 20 LinkedIn connection requests using template A
- Day 7–10: Respond to accepts, send template B (post-connection message)
- Day 8–12: Follow-up sequence to non-repliers (templates C, D, E)
- Target: 2–3 discovery calls booked

**Week 3 — Discovery calls (4–6 hours)**
- Run calls using the existing Phase 5 sales playbook
- Focus: learn what's actually broken for them; don't try to close on call 1
- End-of-week: 1–2 second calls booked with interested prospects

**Week 4 — Close the first one (6–10 hours)**
- Pitch using pricing sheet (artifact 6)
- Signed via DocuSign or simple email-acceptance
- Deliver using manual-service runbook (artifact 5)
- Month 1 revenue: £400. Month 1 lesson: priceless.

## Honest caveats

### On the trademark
"Intel Force OS" has a theoretical Intel Corp conflict. Three-word distinctiveness + class 9/42 + "OS" suffix make it defensible, not bulletproof. Spend £150 on a 30-minute CITMA attorney call before filing. If they say "will get opposed," pivot fast — you've invested 4 weeks, not 4 years. If they say "file it, defend positioning" — proceed.

### On the ICP
The ICP is *"UK SMEs on Breathe HR with 20–200 employees and a human HR/Ops leader."* This is a guess shaped by: your existing Breathe HR integration, solo-founder sales math, the assumption that companies <20 employees don't have enough HR volume to justify £400/month. It might be wrong. Adjust after calls 3–5.

### On the price
£400/month founder tier is a guess. The real price is "whatever the first three customers agree to without balking." Treat £400 as an anchor, not a commitment. If the first prospect says "sure" immediately, you underpriced. If three prospects say "ouch," you overpriced.

### On the timing
30 days to close one customer is optimistic for UK SME sales. Realistic is 6–10 weeks. Don't beat yourself up if week 4 ends with "one hot lead, no signature yet." That's normal. Two signatures by week 8 is the real target.

### On the model
Manual service layer under a product surface is a classic startup pattern (Zappos started by founder buying shoes from retail stores himself). It works IF you rigorously track the hours and know when to graduate to platform. Rule of thumb: when manual overhead per customer exceeds 6 hrs/week or when you have 3+ customers, Phase 3 infrastructure starts earning its keep. Before that, infrastructure is procrastination.
