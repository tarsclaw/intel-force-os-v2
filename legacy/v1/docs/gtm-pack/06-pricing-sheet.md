# 06 — Pre-Platform Pricing Sheet

**One price. One product. No tiers. This is the founder-led, manual-service phase. Simplicity is the feature.**

---

## The price

# £400 per month

- **Billed monthly, cancel anytime** (no annual lock-in at this stage)
- **Includes everything:** agent configuration, prompt tuning, all of Maddox's time, weekly reports, escalation handling, Slack access, monthly review calls
- **No setup fees**
- **Founding customer terms:** first 10 customers locked at £400/mo for 12 months — this price will rise when the product graduates to the platform tier structure

## Alternative annual option (offer if asked)

# £4,000 per year
*(£400 × 12 = £4,800, so this is a ~17% discount — effectively 2 months free)*

- Paid annually upfront
- Same cancellation rights (but partial refunds only if service materially breaks)
- Worth offering to prospects who mention budget cycles or prefer annual procurement
- **Don't lead with it** — monthly is the default, annual is optional

## What's included at £400/mo (what you actually say on the call)

When asked "what do I get for £400":

> "You get the HR agent handling your inbox end-to-end, with me personally configuring and monitoring it. Specifically:
>
> - The agent reads every incoming HR message across the channels you point it at — Breathe HR, email, Slack
> - It drafts a reply to every one, using your handbook and company tone
> - You approve, edit, or reject each draft in about 10 seconds per message
> - Anything sensitive — grievances, mental health, resignations — it escalates to you instead of trying to handle
> - I'm monitoring everything in the background, tuning the agent each week, catching anything it gets wrong before you see it
> - You get a written weekly report every Friday with volume, quality, and what I changed
> - We meet monthly for 30 minutes to review
> - Shared Slack channel for questions during UK business hours — I respond within 2 hours
>
> You're not paying for the technology — you're paying for a managed service that reduces your HR admin load. The tech is how we deliver it, not what you buy."

## How to answer common price questions

### "Why £400?"
> "Honestly — it's the price where the economics work for both of us. I'm pricing it as a managed service, not a SaaS tool, because right now it's a managed service. When we move to self-serve platform later, pricing will look more like tiers. Founding customers like you lock in £400/mo for 12 months regardless."

### "That seems expensive for a chatbot"
> "It would be if it was just a chatbot. What you're actually getting is closer to a part-time HR assistant — for £400/mo versus £1,500+/mo for someone on PAYE. If the agent handles 40–60 messages a week and escalates the 3–4 that need you, that's 10+ hours/week of your time back. The calculation there is ~£25/hour of HR leader time saved, which is below your actual hourly cost."

### "Can you do £250?"
> "Not at this stage. £400 is already below where the platform will price long-term — I'm running this with a heavy manual-service layer, which means most of what I'm charging covers my own time on your account. Below £400, I can't deliver the quality I'm willing to put my name to.
>
> What I can do: if annual cash flow helps, £4,000 for the year — effectively 2 months free."

**DO NOT agree to £250.** Once you set a price below £400 for customer #1, every subsequent customer will benchmark against it. Better to lose a prospect than to lose pricing power across your whole pipeline.

### "Can we try it free for a month?"
> "I don't do free trials at this stage — and I'll tell you why. Onboarding takes me 5–8 hours regardless of whether you're paying. If you're not paying during that time, you're not incentivised to give me the access I need to make it work, and I'm not incentivised to fix the last mile if you're on the fence about staying. 
>
> What I can offer: first month is month-to-month, cancellable with 7 days notice. If after 30 days it's not working, no hard feelings, no long goodbye. Lowers the risk without creating the wrong incentives."

### "Can we do a pilot with a smaller scope?"
> "Yes — if by 'smaller scope' you mean pointing the agent at fewer channels initially (say, just Breathe HR and not email at first), that's actually sensible. Price stays at £400 because my time stays the same. But scope-phasing over the first month is totally reasonable."

**The pitfall to avoid:** discounting for "limited pilots." Customers who expect cheap pilots expect cheap everything. Full price, possibly limited scope.

### "What if we want multiple agents for different teams?"
> "At the current stage, one agent per company. When we move to the platform — probably Q3 — we'll have multi-agent setups for larger orgs. For now, if you want different agents for HR vs Sales Ops, that would be two separate engagements at £400 each. But honestly — start with HR, get it working, then we can have that conversation in a few months."

### "What about SLAs, contracts, all that?"
> "For the first 10 founding customers, we're working on a straightforward service agreement — 3 pages, not 30. When we graduate to the platform tier, we'll have proper MSA/DPA/SLA documents. For now: £400/mo, 7-day cancellation, GDPR-compliant, 99% uptime target on the agent but I'm not committing to a formal SLA with credit-back at this stage. If it's materially broken, I'll refund. That's the honest version."

### "Are you VAT registered?"
> "[Your actual answer — check with your accountant]. If yes: £400 + VAT. If no: £400 flat until we cross the £90k threshold, at which point registration kicks in and the price will add VAT."

**Note:** if you're not VAT-registered yet, tell prospects upfront. Many UK businesses won't hassle you about it (they can't reclaim VAT they weren't charged), but when you do register, existing customers suddenly seeing VAT feel blindsided unless you've prepped them.

## What you're NOT including at £400 (scope discipline)

Be explicit about what £400 doesn't cover. Protects you from scope creep.

### NOT included
- **Data migration from other HR systems** — extra £500 one-off if required
- **Custom integrations beyond Breathe HR + email + Slack** — extra per integration, quoted separately
- **Handbook writing or policy authoring** — you're augmenting their handbook, not writing it
- **Training their staff on HR best practice** — you're not an HR consultant
- **24/7 support** — UK business hours only
- **On-site visits or in-person meetings** — remote only
- **Custom analytics dashboards** — weekly written reports only at this tier
- **Multiple agents** — one agent per engagement

### Say it clearly, early
In the onboarding call, after scope discussion:

> "Just to make sure we're aligned — my £400 covers one agent, the weekly operational load, and my time. If something lands outside that — bigger integration work, custom analytics, writing policy — I'll scope and quote separately, not silently absorb it. Fair?"

**This conversation is awkward for 30 seconds and saves you months of pain.**

## When to raise the price

### The first five customers: £400/mo
- Founding customer status, 12-month rate lock
- These are your references and case studies
- Don't negotiate down, and don't push up

### Customers 6–10: £400/mo (still founding tier)
- Same terms as first 5
- These round out the founding cohort

### Customers 11+: £600–800/mo (founding rate ends)
- Explicit message: "Founding customer spots are filled; moving to launch pricing"
- New price reflects real market value and your improved delivery
- Existing customers remain grandfathered on £400

### Platform launch (month 6+): pricing spec kicks in
- Starter £450 / Growth £1,800 / Scale £4,500 (per Phase 5 spec)
- Grandfathered founding customers migrate to Growth at their £400 rate for the remainder of their 12-month lock; renegotiate at that point
- New customers onboarding via self-serve pay Phase 5 prices

## The contract (simple version, for the founding phase)

You don't need the full MSA+DPA+SLA pack for founding customers yet. A single-page service agreement is legally sufficient and commercially honest.

### Service Agreement structure

```
INTEL FORCE OS — SERVICE AGREEMENT

Parties
  Intel Force Ltd, a company registered in England (number XXXXXXXX)
  and [Customer Name Ltd]

Effective date: [date]

Services
  Intel Force Ltd will provide AI-assisted HR inbox management to 
  [Customer Name], including: configuring and operating an AI agent 
  that reads inbound HR messages, drafts replies, and flags sensitive 
  matters for human handling. Delivery includes weekly reporting and 
  Slack-based support during UK business hours.

Fees
  £400 per calendar month, billed in advance on the 1st.
  Payable by bank transfer or Stripe-issued invoice.
  First 10 customers: rate locked for 12 months from effective date.

Term and termination
  Month-to-month with 7 days notice to cancel, given in writing 
  (email acceptable). No cancellation fee.
  Either party may terminate immediately for material breach.

Data protection
  Intel Force Ltd will process personal data under UK GDPR as a data 
  processor on [Customer Name]'s behalf. Full DPA to be issued within 
  30 days. Sub-processors: Anthropic (Claude API), Relevance AI.

Liability
  Intel Force Ltd's total liability under this agreement is capped 
  at the fees paid by [Customer Name] in the preceding 3 months.
  Neither party liable for indirect or consequential losses.

Confidentiality
  Each party will keep the other's confidential information 
  confidential and use it only for the purposes of this agreement.

Jurisdiction
  This agreement is governed by the laws of England and Wales, with 
  exclusive jurisdiction in the courts of England and Wales.

Signed
  For Intel Force Ltd: __________________   Date: __________
  For [Customer Name]:  __________________   Date: __________
```

### What this one-pager trades off vs. the full MSA
- **You gain:** speed to signature (days, not weeks of redlines)
- **You lose:** some legal sophistication around IP, specific warranties, detailed remedies
- **Acceptable trade-off when:** customer is an SME not a large corporate, £400/mo commitment, month-to-month
- **Not acceptable when:** customer >500 employees, annual contract, any regulated industry

For larger customers: use the full Phase 5 MSA (after solicitor finalisation).

## The payment method

### Preferred: Stripe-issued invoices
- Set up when you have Stripe account verified
- Email the customer a Stripe invoice monthly; they pay via link (card or bank)
- Stripe handles reminders, receipts, payment confirmations
- Fee: ~£2-6 per invoice at £400 (negligible)

### Acceptable alternative: Direct bank transfer
- Invoice manually (PDF from a template)
- Customer pays by BACS to Intel Force Ltd bank account
- No processing fees
- Downside: no automated reminders, you chase manually

### Avoid
- PayPal (amateur signal to B2B customers)
- Personal bank accounts (obviously)
- Venmo/Wise (not standard for UK B2B)

### Payment timing
- Invoice 1st of month, due by 14th (Net 14 terms)
- First invoice pro-rated if signing mid-month
- Late payment (no payment by 21st of month) → polite chase email
- Non-payment by end of month → service pause + direct conversation
- **In founding phase, be flexible on timing; in platform phase, enforce Net 14 strictly**

## Founding customer communication

When you pitch price, frame the founding customer status explicitly:

> "I'm currently onboarding the first 10 customers to Intel Force OS as founding customers. £400 per month, month-to-month, cancel anytime. Founding customer rate locks you in at £400 for 12 months — when we move to platform pricing in a few months, existing customers stay on £400, new customers will see higher pricing.
>
> In exchange for the founding rate, I ask for two things: (a) you give me honest feedback, including when it's broken, and (b) if it's working 3 months in, you're open to being a reference for one or two other prospects — with a testimonial quote at minimum, a case study at maximum. That's the whole deal."

This:
- Anchors the price point clearly
- Creates explicit scarcity (only 10 spots)
- Provides justification for the price (founding status, not just "our price")
- Frames the exchange transparently (cheaper rate for references + feedback)

**Do not have a founding customer exchange be "cheap rate for anything" — specify what you want. Unclear asks get unclear outcomes.**

## The signal to raise price

You know it's time to raise price when 3+ of these are true:

1. You've closed 5+ customers at £400/mo with little price objection
2. Discovery calls close-rate is above 40% (too high means you're underpriced)
3. Customers are giving unprompted positive feedback about ROI
4. You've had multiple "can you do more for us?" asks
5. Your operational capacity is the bottleneck, not demand

At that point, raise to £600 or £800 for customer #11+. Signal the move 2 weeks in advance on LinkedIn / newsletter: *"Founding customer pricing closing at end of [month]. Launch pricing kicks in from [date]."*
