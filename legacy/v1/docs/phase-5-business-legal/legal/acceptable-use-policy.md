# Acceptable Use Policy

**The boundaries of how Clawd may be used. What's permitted, what's prohibited, and what happens when this policy is breached.**

> **Audience:** every Clawd customer (as a schedule to the MSA); internal team deciding when to enforce.
>
> **Status:** v1.0. Plain-English policy, enforceable via the MSA. Should be reviewed by a UK commercial solicitor but less sensitive than MSA/DPA.
>
> **Enforcement posture:** firm on genuine abuse; patient with edge cases. Every enforcement action is a commercial event as well as a legal one.

---

## 1. Purpose

This Acceptable Use Policy ("AUP") is Schedule 3 to the Master Services Agreement. It describes how the Service (Clawd) may be used and what uses are prohibited.

Clawd is a platform for automating professional business operations — proposals, content, lead research, client reporting, and similar workflows. This AUP helps ensure the Service is used responsibly, lawfully, and in a way that doesn't harm Clawd, other customers, or third parties.

---

## 2. What you may do

Customer may use the Service for:

- Running the Agents on Customer's own legitimate business operations
- Processing Customer's own business data (contacts, calls, emails, documents)
- Generating drafts for review and sending by Customer
- Storing operational content in the Vault
- Connecting third-party business tools as integrations
- Giving Customer's employees, contractors, and agents appropriate access
- Using Customer's Clawd-issued API keys for programmatic access under the scope granted

Customer may use the Service for commercial purposes — the Service is specifically designed for commercial use and there is no restriction on charging Customer's own clients for work produced with Clawd's assistance.

## 3. What you may NOT do

### 3.1 Illegal or infringing use

Customer may not use the Service:
- To do anything illegal under UK law or the law applicable to Customer
- To infringe the intellectual property rights of any third party
- To process personal data in violation of Applicable Data Protection Law (as defined in the DPA)
- To facilitate fraud, money laundering, or sanctions evasion
- To distribute content that is defamatory, discriminatory, or otherwise unlawful

### 3.2 Harmful or abusive use

Customer may not:
- Send unsolicited commercial messages (spam) using the Service
- Use the Service to harass, threaten, or impersonate individuals or organisations
- Scrape data from third parties in violation of their terms or applicable law
- Attempt to circumvent rate limits, cost budgets, tenant isolation, or other technical controls
- Probe, scan, or test the vulnerability of the Service without Clawd's written authorisation

### 3.3 Misuse of AI output

Because Clawd produces AI-generated output, Customer may not:
- Publish, send, or otherwise rely on AI-generated output without adequate human review
- Represent AI-generated output as solely human-authored when the context materially requires disclosure (e.g., regulated advice, legal submissions, academic submissions where declaration is required)
- Use the Service to generate content that impersonates specific real people (including public figures) without their consent
- Use the Service to produce deepfakes, synthetic imagery/audio/video of real persons, or otherwise deceptive media

### 3.4 Prohibited content categories

Customer may not use the Service to generate, process, or store content that:
- Sexualises minors or is child sexual abuse material
- Promotes violent extremism or terrorism
- Promotes or facilitates self-harm or suicide
- Is non-consensual intimate imagery
- Is designed to harass a specific individual
- Constitutes hate speech or discrimination protected characteristics under UK law
- Is designed to cause harm through malware, phishing, or social engineering

### 3.5 Security and platform integrity

Customer may not:
- Attempt to gain unauthorised access to other tenants, to the platform infrastructure, or to other customers' data
- Use the Service to host malware, phishing content, or unauthorised cryptocurrency mining
- Attempt to disable, degrade, or overload the Service (DDoS, fork-bombs, resource exhaustion)
- Reverse engineer, decompile, or attempt to extract source code of the Service (except to the extent such restrictions are prohibited by law)
- Circumvent authentication, rate limits, or API quotas
- Introduce code into the Service that affects other customers (e.g., through prompt injection designed to exfiltrate data to third parties)

### 3.6 Reselling and redistribution

Customer may not:
- Resell the Service to third parties, except under a specifically-agreed agency partner arrangement
- Make the Service available as a standalone product to Customer's own customers (i.e., customer may use Clawd to do work for their clients — that's the point — but may not give those clients direct Clawd access unless they're separately contracted)
- Use the Service to build a competing product (direct or indirect scraping of the Service's functionality for the purpose of replicating it)

### 3.7 Regulated industry use

If Customer operates in a regulated industry (financial services, legal, healthcare, dental), Customer is responsible for:
- Ensuring Customer's use of the Service is consistent with applicable regulatory requirements
- Performing any necessary additional due diligence before processing regulated data
- Notifying Clawd if Customer's regulatory obligations require specific commitments (e.g., outsourcing notifications, FCA conditions, NHS DSP toolkit)

Clawd is available to discuss regulatory-specific commitments but does not guarantee the Service is fit for any specific regulatory regime unless explicitly stated in writing.

Clawd can be used to produce political content (campaign materials, policy writeups) provided the content complies with applicable election and campaigning law. Clawd does not endorse any political position, does not restrict use based on political viewpoint within legal bounds, and reserves the right to decline to host content that violates the other clauses of this AUP.

### 4.2 Religious and ethical content

Similar position. Use is permitted within legal and AUP bounds.

### 4.3 Adult content

Clawd is not a platform for adult content. Customer may not use the Service to produce or store pornographic material, sexually explicit content, or content primarily intended for adult-content commerce.

### 4.4 Medical and health advice

Customer may use Clawd to produce business operations content for medical and health businesses (e.g., appointment reminders, patient communications, marketing for dental practices). Customer may not use Clawd to produce or transmit medical diagnosis, treatment advice, or other content that constitutes professional medical advice to end patients without:
- Review and approval by a qualified professional
- Disclosure that the content is AI-assisted where required by law or regulator

### 4.5 Legal advice

Same position. Customer may use Clawd for law-firm business operations. Customer may not use Clawd to produce advice to third parties without appropriate professional review and the required disclosures.

### 4.6 Financial advice

Same position. Customer-regulated financial advice must pass through regulated processes.

## 5. AI-specific principles

### 5.1 Everything drafts, nothing sends

Clawd's design principle is that agents produce drafts; humans approve sends. This is not just a product posture — it's a genuine AUP requirement. Customer's use of the Service must maintain meaningful human review between AI output and external transmission for all consequential communications.

"Consequential" means: communications that affect legal rights, commercial commitments, reputation, safety, or compliance. It does NOT mean every individual message — an internal note, a draft proposal not yet sent, a research summary is not "consequential" until it leaves the customer's review.

### 5.2 Attribution

Customer does not have to disclose that content was produced with Clawd's assistance in most business contexts (no more than a customer would disclose using Microsoft Word). Disclosure is required where:
- Law or regulation requires it (academic submissions, certain legal filings, etc.)
- The content's context makes non-disclosure deceptive (e.g., claiming a personal experience that was written by AI)

### 5.3 Model limitations

Customer acknowledges:
- AI models may hallucinate (produce false information confidently)
- AI models may produce biased output
- AI models may produce content that is legally problematic (defamatory, infringing)
- Clawd's agents have tooling to mitigate these risks (validation hooks, escalation, retrieval grounding) but cannot eliminate them

Customer's review process is the final safeguard. Clawd commits to providing tools that aid review (the dashboard, the escalation system, validation hooks). Customer commits to actually reviewing before sending.

## 6. Enforcement

### 6.1 Proportionate response

Clawd enforces this AUP proportionately. Enforcement scales with the nature of the breach:

| Breach type | Typical first response |
|---|---|
| Accidental minor breach (e.g., trigger-happy webhook retries) | Email to tenant owner asking for remediation |
| Moderate breach (e.g., usage patterns indicating scraping) | Written warning, 30-day remediation window |
| Serious breach (e.g., confirmed spam activity) | Immediate partial suspension; remediation required before restoration |
| Severe breach (e.g., illegal content, attempted platform compromise) | Immediate full suspension; potential termination under MSA clause 11.3 |

### 6.2 Steps before suspension

Where feasible, Clawd will:
- Notify Customer of the specific breach
- Provide a reasonable remediation period (usually 30 days, shorter for serious breaches)
- Document the breach and response

### 6.3 Immediate suspension

Clawd may suspend the Service without prior notice in cases of:
- Illegal content (especially CSAM, terrorism content) — mandatory suspension + law-enforcement reporting
- Active security threat to Clawd or other customers
- Material breach of export-control or sanctions laws
- Customer's account being compromised (as a safety measure, not a penalty)

### 6.4 Termination

Persistent, material, or uncorrectable breach of this AUP is material breach of the MSA, entitling Clawd to terminate under MSA clause 11.3. On termination for AUP breach, Customer's Vault export rights under the DPA still apply except where the export itself would be illegal.

### 6.5 Reporting to authorities

Clawd will report to relevant authorities where:
- Required by law (e.g., CSAM must be reported)
- Reasonably necessary to protect individuals from harm
- Required by a valid court order or law enforcement request under proper process

Customer acknowledges this and waives any objection on the ground that Clawd has reported.

## Internal notes — not for customer-facing distribution

### A. Enforcement decision framework

For internal reference when AUP issues arise:

1. **Is there clear evidence of AUP breach?** If not, investigate before acting.
2. **Is the breach intentional or negligent?** Intent shapes tone of response but not the enforcement eligibility.
3. **What's the commercial context?** A £100k customer gets more patience (and more remediation help) than a trial account. This is not a legal principle — it's a commercial reality.
4. **Is there a safe remediation path?** If yes, prefer remediation over suspension.
5. **Are other customers at risk?** If yes, immediate suspension is justified.
6. **Document everything.** Every AUP enforcement decision logs to the audit log. Reviews (solicitor, investor) may inspect.

### B. Pre-approved enforcement patterns

Common scenarios and pre-approved responses:

| Scenario | Response |
|---|---|
| Customer's agent generating spam because a bug in their config | Email: "Please review — appears to be sending too many follow-ups per hour." Help them fix. |
| Customer testing rate limits legitimately | No action; rate limit does its job. |
| Customer deliberately probing rate limits | Email warning; document. |
| Customer uploads CSAM to Vault | Immediate suspension, report to IWF/police, preserve evidence, terminate agreement. |
| Customer attempts prompt injection to exfiltrate data | Investigate, likely suspension, post-mortem. |
| Customer uses service to run an SMS spam operation for a third party | Suspension, remediation required, potential termination. |
| Customer runs a content mill (dozens of autogenerated articles per day, low review) | Email warning about "everything drafts, nothing sends"; may require operational changes. |

### C. When to involve legal

- Any AUP breach that might involve law enforcement
- Any breach triggering a potential termination
- Customer disputes about whether a use was permitted
- Customer refuses to remediate after documented warnings
- Any public complaint or media inquiry about customer's use

### D. Prompt-injection considerations

We have an emerging risk: customer inputs (transcripts, documents) may contain prompt injection designed to make our agents do something other than intended. This isn't a traditional AUP issue — the injecting party often isn't the customer — but it's a real operational concern.

Our response:
- Design agents to be robust against injection (validation hooks, sandboxing, "everything drafts, nothing sends" principle)
- Educate customers on the risk
- Treat customer-conducted prompt injection against Clawd as an AUP breach

### E. Content moderation position

We are explicitly NOT in the business of content moderation for customer output. Customer is responsible for what they send. Our role is to:
- Provide tools that help customers review before sending (the entire dashboard)
- Refuse to host illegal content
- Refuse to assist with specifically prohibited categories (§3.4)

We do not pre-review or moderate customer content. We do not have AI classifiers filtering output (that's Anthropic's problem, and their usage policy applies through our processing).
