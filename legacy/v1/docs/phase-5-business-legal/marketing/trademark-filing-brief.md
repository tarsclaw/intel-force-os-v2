# Trademark Filing Brief

**What to file, where to file it, how much it costs, and when it's worth the money.**

> **Audience:** founder locking the brand name; solicitor executing the filings.
>
> **Status:** v1.0. Action brief, not a filing. Actual filings need legal review.
>
> **Critical note:** I'm not a lawyer. This brief tells you what filings generally exist, typical costs, and a suggested priority order — but a UK solicitor specialising in IP (ideally with SaaS experience) should confirm before money changes hands.

---

## 1. TL;DR

- File UK trademark for `Clawd` in class 9 + class 42. Budget: ~£340–£500 in government fees, 4–6 months to registration, protects the brand in the UK.
- File EU trademark via EUIPO if launching EU customers in year 1. Budget: ~€850 baseline, 6–9 months, covers all 27 EU member states.
- Hold off on US filing until there's a US customer pipeline. USPTO is more expensive and more adversarial.
- Hire a UK IP solicitor for the UKIPO filing (~£300–£800 professional fees). DIY is possible but the cost-benefit skews toward using someone for a one-off filing.

## 2. Pre-filing checks (do these BEFORE you file)

### 2.1 Availability searches

Free searches, 30 minutes of work:

| Registry | URL | What to search |
|---|---|---|
| UKIPO | search.ipo.gov.uk | `Clawd`, `Claude`, `Claud` — exact and phonetic. Class 9, 35, 41, 42 |
| EUIPO | euipo.europa.eu (eSearch plus) | Same searches; full EU scope |
| USPTO | tsdr.uspto.gov | `Clawd` exact match + phonetic |
| WIPO Global Brand Database | branddb.wipo.int | Global sweep |
| Companies House | find-and-update.company-information.service.gov.uk | `Clawd` as a company name |

Document everything. Screenshot every "no match" result. This becomes your "we did our due diligence" record.

### 2.2 Common-law searches

Trademarks don't have to be registered to exist — common-law trademarks arise from actual business use. Search:
- Google for `"clawd"` plus variations (`"clawd ai"`, `"clawd app"`)
- X: @clawd, @clawdai, @useclawd
- GitHub: github.com/clawd, github.com/clawd-ai
- Product Hunt, Indie Hackers
- LinkedIn company search

If you find active businesses using "Clawd" in a related space (software, AI, professional services), get a solicitor's opinion before proceeding. Even one competing user can create registration friction or future infringement claims.

### 2.3 Domain availability

Trademark registrability and domain availability aren't the same thing, but mismatches are expensive. Confirm these are available before filing the trademark:
- clawd.ai
- clawd.co.uk
- clawd.com (if possible)
- clawd.app

If the `.ai` is parked but the trademark is clear, buy the domain before filing — public trademark filings can attract speculators.

## 3. Classes to file

Trademarks are filed by "Nice classification" class — you pay per class. Your choice of classes determines what the mark protects.

### 3.1 Classes that matter for Clawd

| Class | Covers | File? |
|---|---|---|
| 9 | Downloadable software, SaaS | **YES** — core class for our product |
| 42 | Scientific/technological services including SaaS | **YES** — primary class for SaaS in the UK |
| 35 | Business services, advertising, marketing agencies | Optional — consider if positioning heavily on "agency partner" |
| 41 | Education, training | Skip for v1 |
| 38 | Telecommunications | Skip |
| 45 | Legal/security/dating services | Skip |

**UKIPO recommendation:** class 9 + class 42 covers downloadable and hosted software + SaaS delivery. Two classes is the standard filing for a SaaS product.

Adding class 35 bumps cost by one class fee (~£50 UK, ~€100 EU). Only worth adding if "agency/business services" positioning is central to marketing.

### 3.2 UK trademark fee structure (UKIPO, current rates)

- First class: £170 (online, standard application)
- Each additional class: £50
- Series application (minor variations): £70 per mark in the series

Two classes = £220 for the application itself.

Additional considerations:
- Expedited examination: not available in the UK (EU offers similar programmes at extra cost)
- Registration renewal: every 10 years, same fee structure

### 3.3 EU trademark fee structure (EUIPO)

- First class: €850 online (individual application)
- Second class: +€50
- Third+ class: +€150 per additional class

Two classes = €900 for the application.

An EU trademark covers all 27 EU member states in one filing — excellent value if any EU customers are expected. Post-Brexit, the UK is NOT covered — you need both UKIPO + EUIPO for combined UK + EU protection.

### 3.4 US trademark fee structure (USPTO)

- TEAS Plus (strictest): $250 per class
- TEAS Standard: $350 per class

But the US system is "first to use" as well as "first to file" — you have to prove you're actually using the mark in US commerce, with evidence. This gets expensive fast if there's any dispute.

Recommendation: don't file in US until you have US customers or a concrete US launch plan within 12 months.

## 4. Filing route

### 4.1 Direct filing (UK)

Applicant fills out online form at ipo.gov.uk. Government fees as above. No professional fees.

Pros: cheapest route. Total cost = £220 government fees.

Cons:
- If examiner raises objections, you respond alone
- If a third party opposes your application, you have to defend
- Mistakes in classification or specification wording can narrow your protection
- No one reviewing your strategy

### 4.2 Solicitor-assisted filing (recommended)

Hire a UK IP solicitor. They review availability, draft the classification/specification, file on your behalf, and handle any examiner objections.

Typical UK IP solicitor fees for a standard SaaS trademark filing:
- Pre-filing search + clearance opinion: £200–£500
- Filing application + initial prosecution: £300–£600
- Objection/opposition handling: £500–£3,000 per incident (hopefully none)

Total expected: **£500–£1,100** professional fees + £220 government fees = **£720–£1,320 budget**

Pros: expertise. Right specification wording. Someone to handle objections. Opinion on clearance.

Cons: cost (maybe 3–6x vs DIY).

**Recommendation: hire a solicitor for the UKIPO filing.** The £500–£800 uplift buys risk reduction and time. One UK filing error later (scope too narrow, class missing) is more expensive than paying a competent solicitor now.

### 4.3 Trademark attorneys vs solicitors

In the UK, trademark attorneys (regulated by CITMA — Chartered Institute of Trade Mark Attorneys) and IP solicitors both do this work. Trademark attorneys are often cheaper and more specialised. For a single-brand filing:
- **CITMA-registered trademark attorney:** usually cheaper, highly focused
- **IP solicitor:** can handle broader contract/dispute work too; often part of a commercial firm

For the Clawd filing: **CITMA-registered trademark attorney** is the right match. Fees tend to be £400–£700 for a standard UK-only filing.

Finding one: CITMA has a "find an attorney" search at citma.org.uk. Look for attorneys with SaaS/software experience, review their recent published filings in that space.

### 4.4 International extensions (Madrid Protocol)

Once you have a UK registration, you can extend via the Madrid Protocol to additional countries without separate filings in each. Useful when you're ready for US/AU/CA/etc. expansion.

Madrid Protocol fees: ~CHF 653 (≈£580) + per-country extension fees. Cheaper than separate national filings when targeting 3+ countries. Not urgent for v1.

## 5. Specification wording

This is the part most DIY filers get wrong. The "specification of goods and services" is the wording that describes what the mark protects. Too narrow = unprotected; too broad = rejected as over-claiming.

### 5.1 Sample UK specification (solicitor should refine)

**Class 9:**
> "Downloadable computer software for business process automation, artificial intelligence applications, and workflow orchestration; downloadable computer software for managing customer relationships and generating business communications."

**Class 42:**
> "Software as a service (SaaS) featuring software for business process automation; providing online non-downloadable software for artificial-intelligence-assisted drafting, document management, and business-communication workflows; platform as a service (PaaS) featuring computer software platforms for integrating third-party business applications."

This is illustrative. A trademark attorney will refine wording based on:
- Recent UKIPO examiner practice (what gets objected to and what doesn't)
- Scope of your actual product functionality (don't claim what you don't do)
- Coverage against competitors' filings

### 5.2 Specification pitfalls to avoid

- Using marketing terms ("revolutionary", "innovative") — these aren't goods/services
- Filing for "artificial intelligence" broadly — too vague to register
- Copying another company's specification verbatim — examiner may notice
- Missing technology that's part of the product — you can't expand post-registration without a new filing

## 6. Timeline

Typical UKIPO:
- Day 1: file application
- Days 1–20: formal examination (does the application meet UKIPO formal requirements?)
- Day ~21: publication in the trade marks journal (if no formal issues)
- Days 21–80: two-month opposition window (third parties can oppose)
- Day ~90: registration if unopposed
- Total: ~3 months for unopposed filing

With a solicitor: similar timeline; solicitor handles any examiner correspondence.

EUIPO: similar in pattern, usually 4–6 months total for unopposed.

USPTO: more variable, 8–18 months typical.

**Practical implication:** Clawd can use the name publicly from day 1 (trademarks protect you even during pending examination). The mark applies retroactively to filing date if successfully registered. Launch the landing page the week the filing goes in, not the month the registration arrives.

## 7. What a trademark does and doesn't protect

### 7.1 What it protects

- Use of your exact mark by others on the same/related goods/services
- Use of confusingly similar marks (e.g. "Clawd.AI" if someone else launches "CloudAI" in SaaS)
- Infringement by competitors in your registered classes

### 7.2 What it doesn't protect

- The underlying technology (that's patents, which we don't file)
- Branding beyond the mark itself (that's copyright for designs + trade dress)
- Domain squatting (handled separately via UDRP disputes)
- Use in unrelated industries (Clawd Dental Practice Ltd could register — different class)

### 7.3 What happens after registration

- You get to use ® (registered trademark symbol)
- Before registration, you can use ™ to indicate unregistered mark claim
- 10-year renewal required to keep active
- Monitor for infringement; enforcement is the trademark holder's responsibility

## 8. Budget summary

For a standard UK-only launch (MVP and first-year scope):

| Item | Budget |
|---|---|
| Pre-filing searches (DIY) | £0 |
| CITMA trademark attorney — filing fees | £500–£800 |
| UKIPO government fees (2 classes) | £220 |
| Domain registration (3 domains, 2 years) | £150 |
| **UK launch package total** | **£870–£1,170** |
| EUIPO filing when ready (2 classes) | £850 (government) + £500–£1,000 (attorney) |
| USPTO filing when ready (2 classes) | $500 (government) + $800–$2,000 (attorney) |
| **Full coverage (UK + EU + US) when ready** | **£2,800–£5,500 total, across 12 months** |

Recommendation: spend £870–£1,170 now on UK filing. Defer EUIPO to 6 months after launch (once there's revenue to justify). Defer USPTO until US pipeline exists.

## 9. Defensive registrations (consider, don't rush)

### 9.1 Variants worth considering later

- `Clawd AI` or `Clawd.AI` as a separate mark
- `Use Clawd` if adopted as a call-to-action
- Stylised/logo version (after logo is finalised)

These aren't urgent. Most trademark protection flows from the word mark alone. Add variants only if a specific business reason emerges.

### 9.2 When NOT to register defensively

Don't register variants just because you can. Each additional mark costs time + money to maintain. Register what you actively use.

## 10. Monitoring post-registration

Once registered, consider:
- Set a Google Alert for "clawd" infringements
- Register for WIPO's trademark watch services (fee-based, optional)
- Retainer with trademark attorney for watch-and-enforce (~£500/year, optional)

First year: watchful DIY monitoring is fine. As the brand becomes established, professional watch services become worth the spend.

## 11. Companies House note

Separate from trademarks. If you want "Clawd" as a visible trading name on invoices and contracts:

- Option A (simpler): Intel Force Ltd registers "Clawd" as a trading/business name — no Companies House action needed; just note on invoices ("Intel Force Ltd trading as Clawd")
- Option B: register "Clawd Ltd" as a new company at Companies House (~£12), make it a subsidiary or rename the operating entity

**Recommendation: Option A** for MVP. Less paperwork, same external effect. Revisit at Series A / major commercial milestone.
