## §1 — Definitions

Three distinct end-states for v1.0:

### KILL

**Cease v1.0 development.** The wedge is invalidated by structural evidence. Existing pilots receive 30-day wind-down notice; assets liquidated or open-sourced; refund obligations honoured per pilot agreement. Founder pivots to next initiative.

**KILL is reversible only as v1.1 or v2.0**: a wholly new initiative under different premises, with its own kill criterion. v1.0 itself does not resurrect.

### PAUSE

**Freeze v1.0 development for N weeks while data is gathered.** Existing pilots continue in read-only mode (agents that have shipped stay operational; no new agents ship). Founder + Jack conduct strategic review and define an unfreeze criterion.

**PAUSE has a defined exit:** either unfreeze (resume v1.0 from the slice that was active when paused) OR escalate to KILL/PIVOT if the strategic review concludes the trigger is structural rather than temporary.

### PIVOT

**Rescope v1.0.** The wedge holds but its assumed shape is wrong. Common pivots:

- **Vertical pivot:** recruitment → accountancy, insurance, or another UK SME services domain
- **ATS pivot:** Bullhorn-first → Vincere-first, Voyager Infinity-first, or vertical-without-ATS
- **Deployment pivot:** SaaS → self-hosted, or vice-versa
- **Customer pivot:** consultant-facing → operations-team-facing → owner-facing

**v1.0 commitments to existing pilots are honoured under original scope.** New v1.0 contracts under rescoped terms. The master brief and Ultraplan are amended by atomic-correction commit; Codex ratifies before v1.0 resumption.

**Threshold:** Diagnostic agent does not render cleanly via `cortextos-ifos render-agent diagnostic --tenant <slug>` by end of Week 3 (2026-06-14). "Cleanly" means: renderer exits 0, no `ESC_RENDERER_FAILED` rows in `decision_log`, validate.sh passes against all three fixtures.

### Trigger 3 — JANITOR-BULLHORN-AUTH-W5 (PIVOT)

**Threshold:** Janitor agent cannot authenticate to Bullhorn via the documented OAuth flow (per `bullhorn-integration-path.md` §4.5 refresh-loop architecture) by end of Week 5 (2026-06-28). Authentication failure modes that trigger: (a) OAuth token endpoint returns non-2xx persistently; (b) refresh-loop architecture fails at 10-minute TTL boundary; (c) Bullhorn rate-limits IFOS's auth endpoint preventing pilot operations; (d) Bullhorn partnership programme requirement blocks production-tenant access.

**Owner:** Founder + Claude Code. Weekly check during Weeks 3-5.

**Source:** `sequencing-target.md` §6.6 failure condition (ii); Risk #2 in `docs/RISK-REGISTER.md`; `bullhorn-integration-path.md` Sub-decisions A + B (currently Proposed pending Sunday/Monday commercial conversations — which themselves have not happened due to design-partner-pipeline gap).

- **Option A:** Vincere-first. Mid-market UK recruitment ATS; smaller market than Bullhorn but more accessible API.
- **Option B:** Voyager Infinity-first. UK-native ATS; smaller market; alternative auth model.
- **Option C:** ATS-agnostic with manual data sync. Reduces v1.0 to read-only agent operation against ATS export files; loses much of the Janitor + Concierge value but unblocks pilot acquisition.

Rescope plan attached to PIVOT decision; existing pilots receive 14-day notice with rescope explanation.

**Recovery path:** PIVOT is structural; resumption is via rescoped v1.0 under new ATS assumption. Pivoted v1.0 continues with adjusted master brief / Ultraplan; atomic-correction commit lands the rescope.

### Trigger 4 — TWO-SCOPE-CUT-ACTIVATIONS (PAUSE)

**Threshold:** Two instances of "scope cut" activations during v1.0 build. A scope cut is defined as: the v1.0 agent fleet's planned count (6 agents per master brief §8.2: Diagnostic, Janitor, Scribe, Cash Conductor, Sourcing Scout, Concierge) is reduced by founder decision to <4 agents at any point. Each reduction event counts as one activation; second reduction triggers PAUSE.

**Owner:** Founder; logged in `.agents/decisions/` when activated.

**Source:** `sequencing-target.md` §6.6 failure condition (iii); master brief §12 Risk #4 (Hire #1 mitigation calls for scope cut from 6 → 4 agents but explicitly warns against further cuts).

**Action:** PAUSE state. Rationale: two scope cuts indicates either (a) the v1.0 wedge is structurally too narrow to be valuable, or (b) build velocity is fundamentally below the rate v1.0 requires. Either way, continuing to ship more agents is the wrong move; strategic review needed.

**Unfreeze criterion:** Strategic review by Founder + Jack within 2 weeks of PAUSE activation produces one of three outcomes:

1. **Unfreeze with scope:** restored to 4 agents (no further cuts); resume with adjusted timeline.
2. **Escalate to PIVOT:** rescope vertical or customer-type; pivoted v1.0 has new agent fleet definition.
3. **Escalate to KILL:** the wedge as designed cannot ship even at reduced scope.

### Trigger 5 — AUTOSEND-RED-MISCATEGORISATIONS (PAUSE)

**Threshold:** More than 3 confirmed red-tier autosend breaches per pilot per week from actions that should have been classified as green or yellow. "Confirmed" means: tenant operator files a `false-block` feedback report via Brain UI (or equivalent v1.0 manual channel) AND IFOS oncall agrees the tier classification was wrong. Threshold measured per individual pilot over rolling 7-day windows.

**Owner:** IFOS oncall + tenant operator. Daily check via `decision_log` queries: `SELECT COUNT(*) FROM decision_log WHERE phase='gating_failed' AND payload->>'tier'='red' AND payload->>'block_reason'='red_tier_classification' AND tenant_slug=? AND created_at > NOW() - INTERVAL '7 days'`.

**Source:** `docs/decisions/autosend-safety-policy.md` §5 + §10; Risk #3 in `docs/RISK-REGISTER.md` (LUKS manual unlock has higher impact but autosend miscategorisation has higher frequency).

**Action:** PAUSE for the affected pilot. The agent that produced the miscategorisations halts at next session boundary. Founder + Jack review the policy tier classifications within 48 hours. Policy revision (via Codex ratification) lands as a new commit; affected pilot receives incident report. Pilot resumes after policy revision.

**Escalation path:** If three pilots experience this trigger within one quarter, escalate to system-wide PAUSE while structural autosend policy redesign happens (potential v1.1 advancement of orange tier).

### Trigger 6 — UNIT-ECONOMICS-COST-PER-ACTION (PIVOT)

**Threshold:** Cost-per-completed-action (defined as: total IFOS infrastructure + API + Claude inference cost ÷ count of `decision_log` rows with `phase='action'` and `payload.tier IN ('green','yellow','orange-approved')`) exceeds **£0.50 per action** after 4 tenant-weeks of operation.

**Operational-guess threshold; pilot data overrides.** Tune after first 100 pilot actions provide real cost-per-action data. Pre-pilot rationale: £0.50/action × ~200 actions/tenant-month = £100/tenant-month gross cost; viable only above £500/tenant-month revenue. If first-pilot data shows on-target spend, tighten threshold to ~£0.10/action (current £0.005 estimate × 20x safety margin).

The £0.50 figure assumes:

- Claude Sonnet 4.6 inference at ~£0.003 per action (small prompt, output cached)
- Bullhorn API call at <£0.001 per action (free tier)
- Hetzner infrastructure at ~£8/mo / ~10k actions/mo ≈ £0.0008 per action
- Total: ~£0.005 per action would be on-track; £0.50 is 100x over-target

**Owner:** Founder; monthly check.

**Source:** Master brief §10.4 cost target; emerging from v1.0 pilot operations.

**Action:** PIVOT — likely vector: cheaper Claude model tier (Haiku 4.5 for routine actions, Sonnet for complex), or reduced agent run frequency (e.g., Sourcing Scout runs nightly batch instead of real-time), or per-tenant cost passthrough in pricing.

**Recovery path:** Rescoped pricing/architecture; pivoted v1.0 continues.

### Trigger 7 — INFRASTRUCTURE-COST-MONTHLY (PIVOT)

**Threshold:** Total Hetzner monthly infrastructure cost exceeds **£100/month at 3 active tenants**.

**Rationale:** 15x headroom from the current £6.40/month single-tenant baseline (per Day-4 runbook §1.4: CX22 + 50 GB volume + backups = €5 + €1.20 + €2.40 ≈ £6.40). At 3 tenants on shared single-server architecture, expected cost should remain near baseline. If Postgres + LUKS-volume growth + vector-index growth pushes >£100/month at 3 tenants, infrastructure approach needs pivot.

**Owner:** Founder; monthly check via Hetzner billing.

**Source:** Master brief §10.4 cost target (£20/mo single-tenant pilot); Risk #4 in `docs/RISK-REGISTER.md` (Hire #1 delays compound infrastructure spend if architecture sprawls).

**Action:** PIVOT — likely vectors:

- **Self-host:** founder's Mac Studio cluster from prior research as primary; Hetzner as backup. Saves money but adds maintenance.
- **Provider switch:** alternate eu-central provider (Mythic Beasts, OVH, Scaleway) with better price-per-tenant economics.
- **Architecture compaction:** per-tenant resource pooling rather than per-tenant servers.

**Recovery path:** Rescoped infrastructure plan; pivoted v1.0 continues with adjusted master brief §10.4.

### Trigger 8 — GATE-B-REVENUE-UPLIFT (KILL)

**Threshold:** After 3 completed pilots (defined as: pilot ran for ≥90 days with active agent fleet and quarterly Gate B revenue measurement), average Gate B revenue uplift per tenant <£20k/year.

**Owner:** Founder; assessed at end of each pilot.

**Source:** Master brief §6 Day 5 line 486 — canonical example trigger from master brief.

**Action:** KILL. Rationale: the wedge does not produce demonstrable ROI at the threshold required to sustain v1.0 commercially. £20k/year/tenant is the breakeven floor — below this, IFOS cannot fund continued operation at v1.0 pricing.

**Recovery path:** None within v1.0. Lessons feed v1.1 if launched. The vertical assumption (UK recruitment) or product assumption (agent fleet vs. point tools) may need reconsidering for any next initiative.

### Trigger 9 — CORTEXOS-PRIMITIVE-CHRONIC-FAILURE (PAUSE)

**Threshold:** cortextOS primitives 3 (file bus per `cortexos-primitive-status.md` line item 3) or 4 (approval gates per primitive 4) report >1 production incident per week across **2 consecutive weeks** for any single pilot.

**Threshold tightened Day 5 (2026-05-18) from 4 weeks to 2 weeks.** Rationale: Risk #1 severity was downgraded Day 1 because primitives 3 + 4 confirmed "shipped and tested" per `cortexos-primitive-status.md`. If field evidence shows failure-rate spike for 2 consecutive weeks, that is an actionable signal — waiting 4 weeks delays remediation by 50% within the v1.0 build timeline (Weeks 1-13).

**Owner:** IFOS oncall; weekly check via `decision_log` + cortextOS daemon logs.

**Source:** Master brief §12 Risk #1; Day-1 audit results in `cortexos-primitive-status.md` (primitive 1 PTY/PM2 is "shipped but flaky"; primitives 3 + 4 confirmed "shipped and tested" but field evidence may diverge).

**Action:** PAUSE. Rationale: cortextOS primitive instability is a structural threat to every IFOS agent — every agent uses the file bus and most use approval gates. Chronic failure points to either (a) cortextOS regression upstream OR (b) IFOS usage pattern incompatible with cortextOS at scale.

**Unfreeze criterion:** Either (1) cortextOS upstream PR lands the fix (per master brief §3.1 cross-vertical primitive process), OR (2) IFOS vendor-patches via `packages/harness-patches/` per master brief §3.1 fallback path. Unfreeze after **2 consecutive incident-free weeks** (matched to tightened threshold).

**Escalation path:** Persistent failure after both PR and patch attempts triggers structural review — potential PIVOT to alternative runtime (different harness).

### Trigger 10 — PII-LEAKAGE-INCIDENT (KILL)

**Threshold:** ANY confirmed incident in which agent action results in PII (personally-identifiable information per UK GDPR Art. 4(1)) transmitted to an unauthorised party (cross-tenant recipient, unauthorised external recipient, or recipient outside tenant's declared data residency) AND not blocked by the RLS + autosend policy combination.

**"Confirmed" means:** the incident is documented in `.agents/incidents/<date>-pii-leakage-<n>.md` with: recipient identity, data classes transmitted, attack vector (or accidental cause), and reproduction steps. False alarms (e.g., recipient was authorised but operator was unaware) do not trigger.

**Owner:** Founder + IFOS oncall. Immediate (within 1 hour of detection).

**Source:** Master brief §1 Rule 5 (honest signal before optimistic projection); UK GDPR statutory obligations; Risk #3 in `docs/RISK-REGISTER.md` (multi-tenant trust foundation).

**Action:** KILL. Rationale: multi-tenant trust is the structural foundation of v1.0. A single confirmed PII breach beyond the RLS + autosend boundary indicates the foundation is unsound. Continuing operation risks (a) further breaches, (b) regulatory action, (c) catastrophic loss of pilot trust. Wind-down protects existing pilots from further exposure.

**Recovery path:** None within v1.0. Mandatory ICO notification per UK GDPR Art. 33 (within 72 hours of discovery). Lessons logged comprehensively. Any v1.1 must be architecturally distinct (e.g., per-tenant physically-isolated infrastructure rather than shared Postgres with RLS).

## §3 — Decision authority

**Day-5 restructure** (2026-05-18): Jack is high engagement on sales/commercial; founder is solo on product. This v1.0 kill criterion governs the **product domain**. Founder has SOLO authority for all trigger activations; Jack is informed-not-deciding. Commercial-domain kill criteria are deferred to a future artefact (placeholder: `v1.0-kill-criterion-commercial.md`).

### §3.1 — Product domain (all 10 triggers in this document)

**Founder has SOLO authority for kill/pause/pivot decisions** on every trigger in this document.

- For all PAUSE actions: founder decides; activates within 24h of trigger detection.
- For all PIVOT actions: founder decides; activates within 24h.
- For all KILL actions: founder decides; activates within 24h (within 4h for Trigger 10 PII per §3.5 emergency authority).
- Jack is **informed-not-deciding** via written brief within 24h of any trigger fire. Jack's input may inform follow-on commercial decisions but does not gate the product decision.
- All decisions logged in `.agents/decisions/<date>-trigger-<n>-decision.md` per existing convention.

### §3.2 — Commercial domain (out of scope for this document)

Commercial-side kill criteria — pilot pricing, customer acquisition strategy, sales pipeline kill thresholds — are out of scope for v1.0 product kill criterion. A future artefact (placeholder name: `v1.0-kill-criterion-commercial.md`) defines them with Jack as decision authority and founder informed. Scope boundary: anything that does not directly trigger product KILL/PAUSE/PIVOT belongs in the commercial document.

### §3.3 — Crossover scenarios (product trigger with commercial implications)

Where a product trigger has direct commercial implications, founder makes the product decision but **consults Jack within 48 hours before final pivot direction is locked**. Examples:

- **Trigger 6 (Unit economics):** founder decides PIVOT direction (cheaper model tier, reduced agent frequency, etc.) → consults Jack on pricing implications before locking → updates Ultraplan pricing plan.
- **Trigger 7 (Infrastructure cost):** founder decides PIVOT direction (self-host alternative, provider switch, architecture compaction) → consults Jack on commercial impact before locking.
- **Trigger 1 (Design partner) PAUSE → KILL escalation:** if PAUSE extends 4 weeks without signed LOI, founder consults Jack on whether to escalate to KILL (commercial assessment of pipeline) or extend PAUSE.
- **Trigger 8 (Gate-B revenue) KILL:** founder decides KILL on revenue evidence; consults Jack on wind-down terms with existing pilots before 30-day notice goes out.

The consultation is **binding-on-direction, not binding-on-occurrence**: founder may activate PAUSE/PIVOT/KILL state immediately without Jack's prior input, then refine direction with Jack's commercial input within the 48h window.

### §3.4 — External advisor (deferred — Week-2 must-fill placeholder)

External advisor name **TBD**. Slot reserved for future tie-break needs that may emerge from commercial decisions or post-pilot scale. **Rationale for deferral:** founder-solo authority in §3.1 means no tie-break is needed for v1.0 product decisions; advisor becomes useful only when joint authority structures emerge.

**Must-fill before first pilot LOI signs.** The pilot agreement may reference an external advisor for dispute resolution per `autosend-safety-policy.md` §10 placeholder language. Founder identifies and engages within Weeks 1-2.

### §3.5 — Emergency authority (Trigger 10 PII-LEAKAGE-INCIDENT specifically)

Trigger 10 escalates regulatory-clock-driven actions. UK GDPR Art. 33 mandates ICO notification within 72 hours of discovery; multi-person consultation cannot delay this clock.

- **Founder unilateral PAUSE immediately** (within 1 hour of detection). No consultation required.
- **Founder unilateral KILL within 4 hours** of confirmed incident. No consultation required.
- Jack INFORMED within 24h via written brief (not required for decision).
- ICO notification, if required, follows the founder's KILL decision; founder may engage counsel before submitting ICO notification (within the remaining clock).
- External advisor (§3.4), once engaged, may be looped in within 24h for post-incident review but does not gate the emergency decision.

### §3.6 — Pre-pilot phase (Weeks 1-2 before first LOI)

In the pre-pilot phase (before any signed LOI), all authority and consultation rules above hold, but the **consultation timing windows can compress to "next reasonable opportunity"** rather than the 24h/48h defaults. Rationale: no pilot commitments yet; reversibility is high; speed of decision matters more than process compliance.

### §3.7 — Post-pilot phase (after first LOI signed)

Once any pilot LOI is signed:

- All authority rules above hold per §3.1-§3.6.
- Pilot notification per state (48h/7d/30d for PAUSE/PIVOT/KILL respectively) per §4.
- External advisor (§3.4) must be filled before first pilot LOI signs.

## §4 — Notification path

First 24 hours after trigger fires.

| Time | Action |
|---|---|
| T+0 (trigger detected) | Trigger detector (Claude Code, IFOS oncall, or Founder) writes incident report to `.agents/incidents/<date>-<trigger-id>.md`. Report includes: trigger name, threshold details, evidence (logs/queries/screenshots), preliminary KILL/PAUSE/PIVOT recommendation. |
| T+0 + 15 min | Founder + Jack notified via Telegram (or chosen private channel). Notification includes: incident report path, summary, recommended action. |
| T+1 hour | Founder reviews incident report; either confirms detector's recommendation OR challenges with counter-evidence. |
| T+4 hours | Joint review call between Founder + Jack (and external advisor if engaged). Decision made: KILL / PAUSE / PIVOT / no-action (false alarm). |
| T+24 hours | Decision logged in `.agents/decisions/<date>-<trigger-id>-decision.md`. Decision includes: trigger details, evidence summary, deliberation summary, action chosen, unfreeze criterion (for PAUSE), rescope plan (for PIVOT), wind-down plan (for KILL). |

### Existing pilot notification

After T+24h decision, existing pilots are notified per pilot agreement notice periods:

- **PAUSE:** 48-hour advance notice (where possible; immediate if security-related). Notification includes: trigger summary, expected unfreeze timeline, what happens to their pilot during PAUSE.
- **PIVOT:** 7-day advance notice. Notification includes: rescope plan, what changes for their pilot, opt-out clause (terminate with pro-rata refund if they disagree with rescope).
- **KILL:** 30-day notice. Notification includes: wind-down plan, refund schedule, data-export plan, recommendation for alternative tools.

### Public communication

For PIVOT and KILL events, founder publishes a transparent post-mortem within 60 days of activation. Pattern: founder's existing public communication style (lessons learned, what we'd do differently, what we got right). Builds reputation capital for next initiative.

For PAUSE events: no public communication unless tenant operations are affected for >4 weeks.

## §5 — Recovery path

### PAUSE recovery

1. Unfreeze criterion declared in T+24h decision document.
2. IFOS oncall + founder monitor criterion daily.
3. When criterion satisfied, joint Founder + Jack sign-off (signature pattern: PR with both names on the resumption commit).
4. v1.0 resumes from the slice that was active when paused (rather than restarting Week 1). Adjusted timeline reflects pause duration.
5. Existing pilots notified of resumption with new timeline.

If unfreeze criterion is not met within the declared timeframe:

- 4-week PAUSE: extend by 2 weeks OR escalate to KILL/PIVOT (joint review).
- 8-week PAUSE: must escalate (cannot remain in PAUSE >8 weeks without revisiting end-state).

### PIVOT recovery

1. Rescope plan replaces affected slices of master brief and Ultraplan. New atomic-correction commit lands the rescope edits (separate from the existing 10-edit atomic-correction manifest — pivot is a wholly new manifest event).
2. Codex ratifies the rescope before v1.0 resumption (mandatory).
3. Existing pilots either accept rescope (continue) OR exit with pro-rata refund (per pilot agreement opt-out clause).
4. New v1.0 contracts (post-pivot) under rescoped terms.
5. Risk register updated to reflect pivoted scope.

### KILL recovery

1. **N/A — v1.0 is wound down.** No recovery into v1.0.
2. Lessons comprehensively logged to `.agents/learnings/v1.0-postmortem.md`. Logging includes:
   - Trigger that activated KILL
   - Evidence at activation time
   - Counterfactual analysis (what would have changed the outcome)
   - Architectural lessons (what to do differently next time)
   - Process lessons (founder + Claude Code working pattern critique)
3. Existing pilots completed per wind-down plan; assets liquidated or open-sourced.
4. v1.1 — if launched — is a wholly new initiative under different premises. Not a continuation of v1.0. Has its own master brief, Ultraplan, kill criterion.

## §6 — v1.0 → v1.1 distinction

**This kill criterion governs v1.0 only.**

### What "v1.0" means

v1.0 is defined by:

- The agent fleet of 6 named in master brief §8.2 (Diagnostic, Janitor, Scribe, Cash Conductor, Sourcing Scout, Concierge)
- The infrastructure stack landed in Day 4 (Hetzner NBG1, LUKS, Postgres 16 + pgvector, RLS-isolated multi-tenancy)
- The renderer architecture per ADR-003
- The autosend safety policy per this Day 5 commit (green + red tiers only)
- The vertical assumption: UK recruitment agencies (per master brief §0 + Product Spec)
- The ATS assumption: Bullhorn-first per `bullhorn-integration-path.md` Sub-decision C
- The deployment model: SaaS via founder-owned Hetzner infrastructure
- The commercial model: per-tenant pilot pricing (per Product Spec, exact terms TBD)

### What changes in v1.1

v1.1 begins when v1.0 is operationally stable with ≥1 pilot operational. v1.1 scope per master brief §9 build sequence + Product Spec:

- Adds 7 agents: Inbound Triage, Brief Decoder, Night Sourcer, Competitor Interception, Client Hunter digest, T5 Supply Chain Auditor, T3 Compliance Watchtower
- Adds Brain UI wiki rendering + entity profile pages + graph view (cytoscape)
- Adds yellow + orange autosend tiers per `autosend-safety-policy.md` §9
- Adds AgentMail integration (Inbound Triage only) per master brief §3.2
- Per-firm LoRA pipeline begins (deferred from v1.0)

### v1.1 kill criterion

**Defined in Week 4+ once v1.0 has at least one pilot operational.** v1.1 kill criterion will incorporate v1.0 learnings + v1.1-specific triggers (e.g., wiki UI usability scores, voice classifier drift rates, LoRA training cost-per-tenant).

This v1.0 kill criterion does NOT govern v1.1. A v1.0 KILL terminates v1.0 only; v1.1 — if launched — is a new initiative under its own framework.

### Crossover scenarios

What happens if a v1.0 trigger fires during v1.1 build?

- v1.0 triggers stop applying when v1.1 is in flight AND v1.0 pilots have been migrated to v1.1 commitments.
- If v1.0 pilots are still on v1.0 commitments while v1.1 build proceeds, BOTH kill criteria apply to their respective scopes.
- Founder + Jack arbitrate any ambiguity; default interpretation favours pilot protection over development velocity.
